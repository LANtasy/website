    About

    Local Victoria-based nonprofit organization, LANtasy, has been running community LANs for over a decade. Last year, we ran western Canada's largest LAN event at Gottacon. It is our mission to throw inclusive, all-ages gaming events. To that end, we present LANtasy 2016.

    LANtasy 2016 is being held March 12/13 at Pearkes Arena in Victoria BC. It is a 200+ seat event that hosts competitive and casual tournaments designed for both new and experienced players. Expect local cosplayers to be in full force and for local vendors to be offering awesome deals to LANtasy participants.

    FAQs

    Have some questions? Here’s some answers.

    What is a LAN Party?

    A LAN party is an event where people bring their own computers and play competitive or casual multiplayer games while connected to a LAN (local area network). The event typically lasts the duration of a weekend and involves many different games and tournaments.

    What should I bring?

    • Identification (We need proof that you’re you!)

    • Bring a Guardian or a signed parental consent waiver if you’re under 17

    • Your Computer (and all applicable cabling)

    • A Single Monitor (and all applicable cabling)

    • A mouse or trackpad/ball

    • A mouse pad

    • Keyboard

    • 30-foot LAN Cable – in excellent condition. Cables will be available onsite for $20 each. (Orange or Pink cable is NOT acceptable at the event)

    • Power cable / Other cabling– For your Monitor / Computer. Bring all cables required for your computer & peripherals!

    • Powerbar – Bring one power bar, preferably with an 8ft cable. Do NOT bring a UPS for your computer to the LAN.

    • PC Headset – Headphones WITH A MIC. Note: Desktop mics and speakers are not permitted in the LAN. Remember that LAN events are quite loud — a headset with excellent sound insulation will make a huge difference for you.

    • Food & Beverage Money – There are nearby food trucks, restaurants and fast food establishments.

    What should I do to get ready?

    Everyone needs to take care of themselves first. Here are some tips that have worked for us:

    Get a good night’s sleep leading up to the LAN. This is crucial. The LAN is open 15+ hours a day so getting a good night’s sleep the week leading up to the LAN, will ensure you can game at your best.

    Eat & Drink – Stay well-fed, stay hydrated to keep going at maximum performance. Remember there are lots of eating options near the venue. Outside food and drink is allowed, but NOT ALCOHOL.

    Arrive as a TEAM – Want to sit with with your friends? Probably a good idea to arrive with them. Teams who arrive together usually are seated faster and given better seating.

    Arrive early – If you want to get going right on time, arrive early. While we will be seating as many LANNERS as fast as we can, you don’t want to get stuck in the back of the line!

    Come out with an open mind – There will be many things to do, games to play, and there is a possibility that there will be new games for you to experience as well as introducing new people to some of your favourite games, meeting new people etc.
